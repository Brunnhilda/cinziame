Redesign of a digital magazine about classical music

The Problem

Classical music and opera enthusiasts are always looking for information about performances and musicians in order to chose the events to attends and the CDs to buy. In France, the specialized magazines are very few and they lack of design thinking which makes their viewing difficult, long and ultimately annoying.

The Solution

Classicagenda needed to be user-friendly, offering the visitor an intuitive and pleasant experience and an easy and fast way to find information

My role

Marketing research, Information Architecture, User Journey, UX design, Wireframes, UI design, A/B Testing, HTML Integration

Tools

Sketch, Photoshop, Marvel, HTML, CSS, Wordpress plugins

Competitive Analysis & Research

I first looked for information about the competitors : their sales, market share, offer of services and business models and analysed it in order to understand their strenghts and weaknesses. If the visibility of the brands was very high, their websites lacked of HMI, UX design and design thinking.

Defining Content & Information Architecture

In order to organize the navigation and information of the website I created a content taxonomy map and a site map to organize the pages.


Wireframing

I decided to organize the content of the website in the simplest possible way, with an intuitive navigation in order to make the user journey easy and pleasant for the users.

I designed the wireframes and the UI design with Sketch. To encourage the users to spend more time on the website, the pages contain many contextual links.

User Interface

I gave a lot of importance to the negative space in order to offer a user a clean and relaxing space for reading. This choice was also made to emphasise the pictures of the shows and the artist that are almost always of high quality, in order to give rythme to the long texts. Big images are also helpful to the comprehension of the reviews expecially when it comes to staged operas and ballets.




Close attention is given to details that have the double goal of rythming the textual content and convey sophistication, a very valued quality in the classical music world.

To encourage the users to spend more time on the website, the pages contain many contextual links.


Showing the number of shares at the beginning and the end of each article is there to prompt the use of social networks.


The website is responsive, in the mobile version only one column is present, in order to maintain clarity and not to distract the reading.


A/B testing

I did different tests on the homepage, simplifying the text in the menu as much as possible.

I also changed the number of articles displayed in the first two columns under the featured article and the position of the ad banners, adjusting them according to Google analytics results.

OPTIMIZATION

To increase the number of visitors of the website, I submitted Classicagenda to Google news. It was refused twice, but at the third time, after the last optimization of the html code, the website was accepted.

OUTCOMES

Since its redesign Classicagenda has established itself as one of the references in classical music, thanks to the quality of its articles and its elegant and user-centered design that makes it stand out from the competitors. Its presence on Google news has increased its reputation and the number of visits of 35% in 4 months, according to google analytics.

Classicagenda has been granted the membership to the International Music Press association and is recommended by the Union of theater, music and dance critics.

Key Learnings

Taking the time to empathize with users helps with understanding and identifying the context, emotions, goals, and motivations that affects a user’s interaction with a product. This is a core value that must be carried over throughout an entire project cycle from discovery to design iteration.
