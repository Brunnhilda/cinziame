Design of an app to find dance partners.


The Problem

It takes two to tango but it's not easy to find a suitable partner.

The Solution

Two2tango is a web app that connects dancers and helps them to find suitable dance partners.

My role

UX/UI design, User research, Persona Creation, User Journey, Information Architecture, User App Flow, Wireframes, UI design

Tools

Marvel, Figma

Competitive Analysis

Only two websites help people find dance partners, one is in French and the other in English. The design of both websites is old-fashioned and not appealing and the interface is not ergonomic. The information required to subscribe and search for a partner is so much that it discourages the sign up.

Research

I did a research on 40 tango dancers in Paris and Amsterdam. I asked them questions about their criteria in terms of partners as well as about the difficulties they have in finding partners. 63 % of the dancers are not comfortable about going to dance alone and 51% of them has already missed a workshop because it was mandatory to have a partner.

Defining Content & Information Architecture

In order to organize the navigation and information of the website I created a content taxonomy map and a site map to organize the pages.

Using the Scrum framework I started to work as a product owner and UX/UI designer with a team composed of a scrum master and 3 developers. I explained them the project on the whiteboard and showed them the user journey, then we discussed together the organization of the database.

Wireframing












Prototyping



As the development started, I designed the wireframes in Figma and introduced them to my colleagues via our Slack workspace.

During the daily stand-up we validated their feasibility or discuss the changes to make.

Together we decided to work first on the sign up, then on search results and then on the user profiles.



Check out the prototype


Branding

The branding is based on the colors dark red and black, which are the colors associated with tango. The name Two2Tango plays on the pronunciation of "two"and "to" which I decided to underline in the logo. Starting from the acronym T2T I designed the pictogram, then I isolated the shape of the 2 and used it in the logotype.


User Interface

For the interface I chosed a clean look and I simplified as much as possible the information on each page, in particular in the forms.





The last page I designed was the homepage that focuses on the CTA in order to encourage the sign up. The page shows also the benefit of dancing together, introduces all the features of the App and gives voice to some satisfied customers.





Development

I gave the developers the designed pages and an interaction map

Key Learnings

The core function of the app is connecting people together, and the secondary function I imagined is to help people find tango events. We decided to incorporate the agenda of events to the first sprint but this complicated the database and started to slow down the developement so, according to the agile method, we decided to set this function aside and stay focused on the main function.

I realize how it's important to analyse any sprint and see if it's possible to split into smaller tasks and sprints.
